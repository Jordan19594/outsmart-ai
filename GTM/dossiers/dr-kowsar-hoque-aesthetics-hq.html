Personalisation Hooks
Hook 1 — Lead With Mechanism, Not Marketing

Dr Hoque is a former vascular surgeon — he thinks in terms of blood supply, tissue oxygenation, and cellular metabolism. Red light therapy works precisely by improving microcirculation and ATP production at the mitochondrial level. Open with the mechanism: "Photobiomodulation drives cellular energetics via cytochrome c oxidase — you'll recognise the tissue perfusion angle immediately, Dr Hoque." This is science-to-science, not a gadget pitch.

Hook 2 — Regenerative Medicine Is His Brand

His clinic explicitly brands itself around regenerative medicine: exosomes, PRP, IPRF, plasma bio-fillers. Photobiomodulation is clinically validated to enhance cellular regeneration, collagen synthesis, and wound healing — it belongs in a regenerative clinic. Position the SUPREME 2.0 as the device equivalent of what he already does with injectables: supporting the body's own healing response at scale.

Hook 3 — Body Treatment Gap

The clinic's body offering is limited (plasma bio-filler for hands/neck, PRP body/joints, chemical peels). The SUPREME 2.0 opens a standalone body skin health, post-surgical recovery, and wellness protocol revenue line. Post-MAFL facelift recovery with RLT is a credible clinical protocol that differentiates the surgical offering and extends the patient relationship beyond the procedure day.

Likely Objections & Counters
Objection 1 — Evidence standards (high bar)

UCL-trained surgeon, GMC-registered — he will require peer-reviewed evidence. Be ready with PubMed citations: photobiomodulation for collagen synthesis, wound healing, and anti-inflammatory effects. Lead with studies most relevant to his surgical background (post-procedure tissue healing, vascular effects of PBMT). Avoid unsubstantiated claims.

Objection 2 — Space and integration

The Kennington flagship is a "private studio" environment — likely compact. A half-body panel needs to fit the room flow. Position it as a pre-consultation skin prep protocol or post-procedure recovery add-on that runs in an existing room between appointments, not a standalone room requirement.

Objection 3 — Patient uptake at a low-volume premium clinic

Aesthetics HQ is doctor-led and deliberately low-volume — not a high-throughput medispa. Prepare a conservative model: 3–4 sessions/week at £75–100 per add-on covers the £899 device cost in 3–4 weeks. Position it as a premium recovery protocol (post-MAFL, post-PRP) at £75–100/session, not a commodity facial add-on.
