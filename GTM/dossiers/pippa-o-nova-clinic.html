Personalisation Hooks
Hook 1 — Upgrade From Celluma to Clinical-Grade

Nova already charges £50/session for Celluma LED — a small curved panel treating one area at a time. The Solis SUPREME 2.0 is a direct upgrade: half-body coverage at clinical irradiance in a single 10–20 minute session. This isn't a new concept — it's making what Pippa already sells five times more powerful, more marketable, and more premium. The membership tier could include SUPREME 2.0 sessions at £75+ vs. the current £50 Celluma.

Hook 2 — Wellness Rebrand Timing

Nova just rebranded as a "luxury cosmetic & wellness space" in February 2026. The SUPREME 2.0 is exactly the device that makes that positioning real: a premium red light therapy panel photographs beautifully, generates social content, signals wellness sophistication, and adds a recovery ritual to the client experience. It's the single device that most efficiently communicates "luxury wellness" to a client walking through the door.

Hook 3 — Post-Procedure Recovery Protocol

Nova's highest-revenue treatments (Endolift £2,500–3,500, eCO2 £800, Genius RF £750/session) all have recovery periods where red light therapy is clinically validated to reduce downtime, accelerate healing, and enhance results. Position the SUPREME 2.0 as a protocol addition that makes Pippa's most expensive treatments even better — and gives her a post-procedure add-on that commands £75–100 per session with zero consumables.

Likely Objections & Counters
Objection 1 — "We already have Celluma LED"

Celluma is a localised, single-area device. The SUPREME 2.0 delivers half-body coverage at clinical photon density — it is a categorically different treatment. Celluma is an add-on; SUPREME 2.0 is a standalone revenue line. Different use case, different patient journey, different price point. And at £899 vs. a Lutronic Genius at £30k+, it's the lowest-cost device in the room.

Objection 2 — Device saturation / room space

Nova already has 10+ devices including EmSculpt, HIFU, cryolipolysis, multiple laser systems. She may feel the clinic is at device capacity. Counter: the SUPREME 2.0 is a panel that can be used in any existing room as a pre- or post-treatment protocol — no dedicated room required. It slots into the existing client flow rather than requiring a room block of its own.

Objection 3 — Credibility vs. consumer wellness trend

Pippa runs a medically-led, CQC-registered clinic. She may be cautious about anything that reads as consumer wellness rather than clinical. Lead with Solis's award-winning status, the photobiomodulation science (which she already posts about on Instagram), and position this as the same modality she already communicates to her clients — just at professional scale.
