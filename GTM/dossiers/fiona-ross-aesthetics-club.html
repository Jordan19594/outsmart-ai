Likely Objections & Counters

Fiona recently used £250k of IFS loan funding for clinic build-outs. A further spend may feel premature. Counter: At £899, this is the lowest-cost device in any of her treatment rooms — compare to Morpheus8 (£30k+) or Moxi. The ROI case is immediate (1 session/day covers the device in 2 weeks at modest pricing). Frame it as revenue infrastructure, not capex.

Objection 2 — Evidence standards

Fiona is Masters-qualified and values clinical evidence — she endorses SkinCeuticals explicitly for being "evidence-backed." Come prepared with peer-reviewed PBM research (collagen synthesis, post-procedure recovery, skin rejuvenation). She will want studies, not marketing claims. Have the Solis clinical evidence pack ready.

Objection 3 — Space in treatment rooms

Notting Hill clinic may have limited floor space. Position the SUPREME 2.0 as compact and room-flow compatible — it can be used as a pre- or post-treatment add-on within an existing room, not a standalone room requirement. The Gleneagles concession and Newton Mearns sites likely have more space.
