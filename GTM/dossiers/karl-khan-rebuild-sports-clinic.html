Likely Objections & Counters
Objection 1 — Evidence scepticism

Karl is HCPC-registered and clinically trained — he reads the research. He will ask for peer-reviewed evidence on RLT for MSK conditions. Come prepared with PubMed citations on photobiomodulation for tendinopathy, muscle recovery, and post-exercise inflammation. Don't lead with lifestyle claims.

Objection 2 — Commercial model question

As a boutique clinic, Karl will want to know how he charges for RLT. Give him a concrete model: 10-min RLT pre/post-session at £25–35, amortising the £1,979 device cost in under 3 months at moderate volume. At 3 uses/day × 5 days = £2,100+/month potential.

Objection 3 — Space / logistics

City of London premium rent means treatment room space is at a premium. The STUDIO 1.0 is a floor-standing full-body panel. Worth asking about layout before pitching — or pivot to the SUPREME 2.0 (half-body) as a more footprint-friendly entry point at £899.
